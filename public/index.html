Background Information

The value of an idea lies in the using of it.

Thomas A. Edison, American Inventor

DatApocalypse aims to sensitize the general public to open data in a fun and interactive way.

What if Australians were using open data more often? Open data can be useful to anyone. Journalists would build stronger stories, primary schools students would be more innovative in their geography project and young couples would find their ideal house more easily.

Open data has been available for a long time but a large part of the public is not aware of the immense resources it can provide for daily decision making but also for Apocalyptic Scenarios. 

By showing resources and giving useful information that users would need in extreme situations, DatApocalypse shows a range of information that open data can provide and where to find it.

 
Scenario Introduction
ZOMBIE ATTACK
The higher the density, the lower the chances of surviving a Zombie attack. Population density will be the most important factor to determine the survival index.
Children should be more careful, Zombies like fresh flesh and will be on the hunt for young humans.
Zombies are attracted by wealthy and luxurious areas, hence why living in an area where the average income is high will be more disastrous.
FIRE BREAK OUT
When a fire breaks out, people are safer in open areas.
In this scenario, the chances of survival are lower in areas where the population density is higher. Wise people are more likely to find escape from a fire big in an open area.
Proximity to a major water source is the most important survival factor.
EARTHQUAKE
Earthquakes tend happen in the same area, history of earthquakes in the selected suburb will have a high impact on survival chance.
However this may be balanced by living in an area where the average income is higher or the number of hospitals is high.
These suburbs tend to be more prepared to earthquake disasters. Smaller and younger individuals are more flexible and will be more able to protect themselves by hiding in small places.
